Tokyo Ueno Park


Ueno Park ( Japanese :上野公園; Roman : Ueno Kōen ) is a large public park in Ueno, Taitō -ku, Tokyo , Japan. The park was established in 1873 on the site of the former Kan'ei-ji Temple as one of the first public parks in Japan. Ueno Park was established in Western style, in part due to the borrowing and adaptation of international practices during the early Meiji period.



Ueno Park was granted permission to be built on imperial land by Emperor Taisho in 1924. Its official name is Ueno Onshi Kōen (上野恩賜公園), meaning "Emperor's Gift Ueno Park". The park is home to several important museums and is also famous in spring for its cherry blossom (hanami) festival. In recent years, Ueno Park and its attractions have attracted more than ten million visitors a year, making it Japan's most popular urban park.



Both foreign and Japanese tourists often visit the museums ( Tokyo National Museum, National Museum of Science and National Museum of Western Art ), the Concert Hall, Shinobazu Pond and Ueno Zoo.



Ueno Park and its surrounding area are well-known and appear in many Japanese novels, and are homeless people living there.
