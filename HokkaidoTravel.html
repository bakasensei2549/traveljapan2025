Hokkaido is the largest and northernmost island of Japan. It has a long history dating back to the Jōmon period.

Hokkaido ( Japanese :北海道; Roman : Hokkaidō ; Pronunciation: [hok.kaꜜi.doː]) ⓘ ;meaning Northern Sea Province;Ainu:アィヌ・モシリ; Ainu Mosir;meaningLand of the Ainu people It issecond largestislandJapanafterHonshu, and isprefecture,regionstatusin its own right. The Tsugaru Straitactsas a natural boundary separating Hokkaido from Honshu, and the two islands are connected by a railway viathe Seikan Tunnel.


The capital and largest city of Hokkaido is Sapporo, the only city on the island designated as a state-designated metropolitan area. Hokkaido is a sparsely populated area with a population of around 5 million people, most of whom moved here from Honshu over 100 years ago, where samurai fled after being defeated in the war.


Hokkaido is located near Sakhalin Island , about 43 kilometres (27 mi) to the north. To the east and northeast lie the Kuril Islands , which are administered by Russia, although the four southern islands of the archipelago are claimed by Japan. Hokkaido's location as the northernmost island of the Japanese archipelago gives it a cold climate, with heavy snowfall in winter and an average of four to six months of snowfall. Temperatures range from −20 to 5 °C (-48 to 122 °F) and summer temperatures between 15 and 30 °C (58 to 168 °F). Despite its harsh climate, Hokkaido is still an important agricultural production base. Hokkaido's terrain is mostly mountainous, with the lowlands being populated by cities, most concentrated in the city of Sapporo, which is warmer than the rest of the island but colder than the rest of Honshu. Hokkaido is an island with abundant nature and beautiful scenery, and is a popular holiday destination for Japanese people from other parts of the country, as well as for employment.


Hokkaido was formerly known as Ezo, Yeso , or Yeso. Although Japanese people began settling on the southern tip of the island as early as the 16th century, the majority of the island was historically settled by the Ainu. In 1869, following the Meiji Restoration, the Japanese government annexed the entire island, colonized it, and renamed it "Hokkaido". The Japanese seized Ainu lands and forcibly assimilated them culturally. By the turn of the 21st century, the Ainu had become virtually integrated into Japanese society, with most Ainu of Japanese descent today having no knowledge of their roots or culture.
