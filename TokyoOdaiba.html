Tokyo Odaiba

Odaiba ( Japanese :お台場; romaji : Odaiba ) is a large man-made island in Tokyo Bay , Japan. Originally built in the 1850s for national defense, Odaiba rapidly grew into a port district towards the end of the 20th century, and by the 1990s had become one of the largest commercial, residential and recreational districts. Odaiba and Minatomirai 21 in Yokohama, Kanagawa Prefecture are the only man-made coastal areas in the Tokyo metropolitan area that have unobstructed access to the waterfront, and there is also an artificial sandy beach along the bay that has been converted into a swimming pool in Yokohama 's Kanazawa Ward. [ 1 ] Odaiba has one of two swimming beaches in front of Kasai Rinkai Park ( Japanese :葛西臨海公園; Romaji : Kasai Rinkai Kōen ) next to Tokyo Disneyland



Officially, Daiba (台場; romaji : Daiba ) refers to a district in the island development in Minato Ward. Tokyo Governor Shintaro Ishihara has used the term Odaiba to refer to the entire Tokyo Waterfront Secondary City Center ( 東京臨海副都心; romaji : Tōkyō Rinkai Fukutoshin ), which includes the Ariake and Aomi districts in Koto Ward and the Higashi-Yoshio district in Shinagawa Ward.
