Hokkaido Furano Flower


Furano ( Japanese :富良野市; Ainu : フラヌイfuranui ) is a city in Kamikawa Sub-prefecture, Hokkaido, Japan . It is a popular tourist destination for its lavender fields and winter skiing.


Furano Flower History

The name of the town comes from the Ainu language, the native people of Hokkaido, "Hura-nu-i" (フラヌイ, hura-nu-i), meaning "place with a smell", possibly because the valley originally smelled of sulfur from the summit of Mount Tokachi.


Furano City


In 1897, people from Mie Prefecture settled in this area (in the present Ogiyama area of the city), and Furano Village was established. In 1899, it was transferred from Sorachi Sub-Prefecture to Kamikawa Sub-Prefecture, and a city hall was officially established after the establishment of a railway line to Asahikawa, the second largest city in Hokkaido, in 1900.

Geography

Furano Valley is located between the Tokachi Volcanic Mountains (part of Daisetsuzan National Park) and the peak of Yubari Mountain

with the Sorachi and Yubari Rivers flowing through the city. Since Furano is located in the center of Hokkaido

it is nicknamed “Heiso no Machi” or “Navel Town

and the Monument of the Center of Hokkaido is located on the grounds of Furano School at 43°20′56″N 142°23′04″E.
