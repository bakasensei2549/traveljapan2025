Tokyo Tower

Tokyo Tower ( Japanese :東京タワー; Tōkyō tāwa ; English : Tokyo Tower ) is a large communications tower in Minato ward, Tokyo , Japan. It is 332.6 metres (1,091 ft) high. It was completed on October 14, 1958. In addition to being a tower for transmitting various television signals such as NHK, TBS , etc., Tokyo Tower is also one of the most important tourist attractions in Tokyo. Over 2.5 million people visit the tower per year . The tower area can be divided into 3 parts. The lowest part is a 4-story building located directly below the tower. Inside, there are museums, shops, restaurants, etc. The remaining 2 parts are observation decks of the tower, located at heights of 150 meters and 250 meters respectively .



May 1957 - Japan Signal Towers Co., Ltd., the company that manages the tower, was founded.

June 29, 1957 - Construction began.

October 14 , 1958 - Construction completed.

December 7 , 1958 - Opened for viewing.

December 23 , 1958 - Officially opened to the public.

January 10 , 1959 - National Television and Educational Services ( NHK ) began broadcasting on VHF Channel 1.

February 1, 1959 - Educational Television Station (NET) or Asahi Television began broadcasting on VHF Channel 10.

March 1 , 1959 - Fuji TV began broadcasting on VHF Channel 8.

April 1959 - The NHK Educational Television transmitting tower was moved to the Kioi transmitting tower, and the main NHK Television transmitting tower was installed in its place.

January 17 , 1960 - TBS 's transmitter tower was moved in (VHF Channel 6)

April 12 , 1964 - TV Tokyo broadcasts on VHF channel 12.

April 26 , 1970 - First broadcast on FM radio TOKYO on FM 80.0 MHz, along with the relocation of NHK-FM's transmitter to the tower on 82.5 MHz.

November 10 , 1970 - NTV's transmission tower was moved and installed (VHF Channel 4)

1980 - NHK Educational Television moved in (VHF Channel 3)

October 1 , 1988 - Radio station J-WAVE began broadcasting on 81.3 MHz.

November 1, 1995 - TOKYO MX began broadcasting on UHF channel 14.

April 1 , 1996 - InterFM began broadcasting on 76.1 MHz

.

July 24 , 2011 - Nine Tokyo-based TV stations ended their analog broadcasting and switched to digital broadcasting from midnight on the same day.

February - July 2012 - Due to the earthquake (this event caused the top of the transmission tower to tilt), all 9 TV stations stopped broadcasting in analog signals and broadcasted in digital signals instead since 2011. As a result, the top of the radio wave transmission tower for analog television was dismantled and the height of the tower was reduced to 315 meters, down from the original 18 meters.

August 10 , 2012 - The company that operates the transmission tower is scheduled to complete the installation of the new radio wave transmission tower on that date (but no announcement has been made regarding future targets for use), bringing the transmission tower back to its original height when it was first built and allowing it to resume use.
