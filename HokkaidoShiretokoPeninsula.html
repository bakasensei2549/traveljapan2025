Hokkaido Shiretoko Peninsula


The Shiretoko Peninsula (知床半島; shiretokohantō ; Shiretoko Hantō) is located at the easternmost point of the Japanese island of Hokkaido, in the Sea of Okhotsk. The peninsula is separated from the Russian island of Kunashir by the Nemuro Strait. The name Shiretoko comes from the Ainu word sir etok , meaning "edge of the land" or "point where the land juts out". The towns of Rausu and Shari are located on the peninsula


On 14 July 2005, at a World Heritage Committee meeting held in the Republic of South Africa, the Committee declared the Shiretoko Peninsula a World Heritage Site under the name Shiretoko , and it was officially inscribed on 17 July. This peninsula is unique in that it is the southernmost point in the Northern Hemisphere where sea ice normally forms.


Geography


The peninsula is lined with volcanoes that stretch from Cape Shiretoko at the tip of the peninsula, such as Mt. Shiretoko, Mt. Unabetsu, and Mt. Io. This volcanic area has many hot springs , such as Seiseki Onsen and Iwaobetsu Onsen. The variety of hot springs makes the peninsula a popular tourist destination.

Local flora and fauna

The Shiretoko Peninsula is home to many species of conifers and broadleaf plants, such as Quercus mongolica. Animals that live in this area include Red Foxes, Amur brown bears, Sika Deer, and most commonly the Sea eagle. Seals and cetaceans can be found along the coast.

Conservation efforts

In 1964, most of the peninsula was designated as Shiretoko National Park to preserve the peninsula's wildlife and pristine natural beauty. Since then, strict rules have been in place to conserve nature, and entry into the park is not permitted.
